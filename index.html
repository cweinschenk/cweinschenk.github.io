About

I currently work for UL FSRI. Before joining UL FSRI, I previously worked at Jensen Hughes and in the Fire Fighting Technology Group at NIST focusing on full-scale and lab-scale experiments designed to improve firefighter tactics and better understand the thermal performance of firefighter personal protective equipment. I am part of the development team of Fire Dynamics Simulator, which is a computational fluid dynamics tool that gives users the ability to model fire-driven flows.

Some recent projects I have worked on include a high-rise fireground study (High-Rise Field Experiments), the use of computer modeling to examine the fire dynamics of firefighter line of duty deaths (LODD in Residential Structure in Chicago, LODD in Residential Structure in San Francisco), development of an interactive map for recent NIST studies (NIST Fire Studies) and a community risk assessment and fire department performance tool (Fire CARES).
